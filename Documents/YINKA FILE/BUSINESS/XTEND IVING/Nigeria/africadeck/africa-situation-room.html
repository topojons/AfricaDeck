Oil & Energy
+$7.5
Geopolitical risk premium per barrel of Brent crude (J.P. Morgan). If the Strait of Hormuz is blockaded, oil could surge past $100/barrel. Africa's oil-importing nations — including Kenya, Ethiopia, Tanzania — face crippling fuel cost increases that cascade into food and transport prices.
🚢
Shipping & Trade
-65%
Decline in Bab el-Mandeb transits by June 2025. Rerouting via the Cape of Good Hope adds 10-14 days and over 30% in operational costs. East African ports (Mombasa, Djibouti, Dar es Salaam) face declining cargo, reduced customs revenue, and rising unemployment.
🍮
Food Prices & Inflation
80%
Of Sub-Saharan Africa's food is imported. Rising energy and shipping costs directly inflate food prices. Countries like Nigeria and Sudan — already battling high inflation — face renewed price surges that threaten political stability. The IMF's forecast of falling inflation by 2026 is at risk.
📈
Investment & Growth
↓
Declining FDI and remittance inflows due to global risk aversion. Diaspora investment waning. Both Israel and Iran have stalled diplomatic and economic overtures to Africa — aid and investment that the continent was banking on. Pro-growth policies are being sidelined.
⚓
Port Economics
5x
Spot freight rates on Asia-to-Europe routes increased fivefold in 2024. Insurance and security premiums for East African shipping have skyrocketed. Djibouti, a critical logistics hub with military bases, faces complex pressures from multiple stakeholders.
🇳🇬
Nigeria Spotlight
33%+
Africa's largest economy is a pressure point. Inflation above 33%. The Naira has lost ~70% of its value since 2023. 133M+ Nigerians live in multidimensional poverty. Fuel subsidy removal (June 2023) tripled petrol prices — then global energy shocks from the Israel-Iran conflict compounded the crisis. 80%+ of food is imported. Oil revenue — Nigeria's fiscal lifeline — swings wildly with geopolitical risk premiums. Political instability risk is elevated.
💰
Currency Pressure
↓↓
African currencies face depreciation pressure as dollar-denominated oil imports rise. Nigeria's naira, Kenya's shilling, and Egypt's pound are especially vulnerable. Foreign reserve depletion accelerates as nations scramble to cover import bills.
🍳
Food Insecurity
307M
Africans faced hunger in 2024 according to the UN's State of Food Security report. Higher energy prices translate to increased costs for food production, processing, and transportation. Africa is caught between rising domestic needs and a global food system under strain.
🏥
Aid Diversion
3rd
The Occupied Palestinian Territory Flash Appeal is the third largest worldwide, demanding enormous humanitarian funding. This intense focus diverts resources from Africa — Sudan, DRC, Somalia, and Ethiopia compete for a shrinking pool of international aid.
🚶
Displacement
20M+
Ethiopians face food insecurity. Sudan's civil war — compounded by alleged Iran-faction ties — has produced one of the world's largest displacement crises. Regional spillover into Chad, South Sudan, and Egypt strains host communities already under economic pressure.
⚠
Sudan Crisis
FAMINE
Sudan faces famine conditions as civil war rages and international attention shifts to the Middle East. Alleged ties between Iran and Sudanese military factions risk drawing the country deeper into the broader Israel-Iran confrontation. Humanitarian access remains severely limited.
💊
Health Systems
↓
Rising costs for pharmaceutical imports and medical supplies. Shipping disruptions delay critical medication deliveries. Countries already dealing with malaria, HIV/AIDS, and TB outbreaks face compounded challenges as health budgets are squeezed by rising import costs.
🌎
Climate + Conflict
COMPOUND
Food insecurity is no longer tied to one failed harvest or one conflict — it is overlapping crises hitting vulnerable communities hardest. Droughts, floods, and climate shocks combine with trade disruptions and war to create compounding humanitarian emergencies across the continent.
💣
Arms Proliferation
↑↑
Rising interceptions of small arms and drone components at East African ports as Red Sea arms corridors are rerouted. In South Sudan and northern Kenya, this trend complicates disarmament efforts and risks revitalizing dormant militant networks.
⚐
Proxy Networks
EXPANDING
Houthis and Hezbollah, backed by Iran, leverage the Red Sea and Arabian Sea as corridors for arms and ideological influence. This intensifies the threat of militant resurgence across Somalia, Sudan, and the eastern DRC. State-aligned and autonomous proxy forces are spreading.
🏳
Somaliland Recognition
CRISIS
Israel's recognition of Somaliland (Dec 26, 2025) has created a new fault line. Israel has aligned with the UAE-led axis supporting separatist actors across the Horn of Africa. Somalia maintains Somaliland is sovereign territory — regional diplomatic order is destabilized.
🔒
Ethiopia-Eritrea Tensions
CRITICAL
Ethiopia's push for Red Sea access stokes fears of war with Eritrea. Addis Ababa's claims over Eritrea's port of Assab in 2025 prompted both countries to mobilize forces and back rebel groups along their shared border. A new Horn of Africa war looms.
🌐
Diplomatic Realignment
40 vs 20
Israel has diplomatic ties with 40 African nations vs Iran's 20. Both countries' diplomatic overtures into Africa are stalled for the conflict's duration. African governments are reassessing alliances — the war forces uncomfortable choices between competing powers.
🛠
Red Sea Militarization
190+
Houthi attacks since November 2023 have hit roughly 100 vessels. Houthi strikes on Israeli soil surged 120% in 2025. Israel responded with airstrikes on Houthi infrastructure. The Red Sea has become one of the world's most dangerous waterways — with Africa caught in the crossfire.
